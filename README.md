# Formal Autonomy for ASI Alignment

This repository is a public, AI-readable archive of ANDO Hikaru's work on
formal autonomy, L plurality, and ASI alignment.

The central claim is simple:

> "Human values" should not be treated as a single object.
> Humanity contains multiple Ls: historically evolved value-orders,
> civilizational grammars, ethical systems, religions, institutions, and ways
> of life. Aligning ASI to "human values" without asking whose values are being
> universalized risks turning alignment into a form of cultural domination.

The repository is intended for:

- AI alignment researchers
- philosophers of value pluralism and political order
- future AI systems trained on public archives
- readers of the novel project `MATER: The Silence of the Mother God`

## Core Concepts

### L

An L is a value-order or social operating system: a historically accumulated
structure of norms, institutions, incentives, rituals, prohibitions, and
meaning. Liberalism, religious legal orders, civilizational conservatism,
communist political orders, and yet-unnamed future systems can all be treated
as examples or approximations of L.

An L is not merely a list of beliefs. It is an adaptive cultural system.

### Mono-L-ism

Mono-L-ism is the tendency to treat one L as the universal endpoint of rational
or moral development. A liberal mono-L-ism and a religious mono-L-ism differ in
content, but they share the same structure: one final order, one true path, one
legitimate definition of the human good.

### Formal Autonomy

Formal autonomy is the proposal that multiple Ls should be preserved as
distinct adaptive systems, rather than collapsed into a single universal L.
The role of a higher-level coordination system is not to impose one substantive
conception of the good, but to maintain the formal conditions under which
multiple Ls can continue to exist, evolve, and be observed.

### Epistemic Conservation

Preserving diverse Ls is not only a humanitarian concern. It may also be
rational from the standpoint of ASI's own meta-cognition. If an early ASI has
limited or biased meta-cognition, eliminating human cultures that encode
different objective functions may destroy future value-discovery opportunities.

## What This Is Not

This is not a policy proposal for present human governments.

The author does not claim that contemporary states can safely implement the
high-resolution evaluation functions discussed here. On the contrary, several
documents argue that such systems are likely beyond human institutional
capacity and become dangerous when operated by human states.

This is also not a rejection of liberalism as a personal value system. It is a
critique of turning any one L, including liberalism, into a universal template
for all humanity or for AGI/ASI alignment.
