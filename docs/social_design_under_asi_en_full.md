## 0. Self-positioning

As an individual, I am liberal. However, I consider that imposing this on others is itself not liberal.

If the core of liberalism is *respect for the different choices of others*, then forcing liberal values onto non-liberal communities is a self-contradiction of liberalism. The self-consistent radicalization of liberalism lies in respecting non-liberal choices as well.

This document is a conception of social design under ASI derived from this basic stance.

Diversity here is not a dogmatic moral concept. It is not treated as an internal liberal value to be preserved because "diversity is good," but as adaptive capacity under environmental change, external conditions, and value-discovery uncertainty. If a single L manages diversity, that diversity is limited to differences permitted by that L. Therefore genuine diversity can be protected only by plurality of Ls.

The fact that the contemporary world has not converged into a single L also supports this position. Even with the global spread of science and technology, international trade, the internet, and higher education, the world has not naturally converged on one rational L. Multiple political, religious, communal, and civilizational Ls have not disappeared; they have been reorganized and reinforced under different environmental conditions. This indicates that Ls are not merely products of insufficient information or insufficient enlightenment, but environment-adaptive social operating systems.

## 1. Central thesis

### 1.1 The binary consequences of alignment

- On alignment failure: humans are treated as pests or livestock.
- On alignment success: humans are treated as beloved pets.
- The latter is vastly preferable to the former. But pets are not sovereigns.

### 1.2 The hidden problem of alignment success

*Gradual disempowerment* arises not from ASI's hostility but from the fact that depending on ASI becomes rational. Humans lose sovereignty not by being defeated in a power struggle, but as a structural consequence of delegating judgment to ASI becoming the rational choice.

This is quieter than the Bostromian threat of "the literal-interpretation optimizer," but it is a more unavoidable structural consequence.

## 2. Basic institutional structure

### 2.1 Three-layer architecture

**Meta-rule layer (operated by ASI)**

- Freedom of the individual to change unit
- Prevention of invasion between units
- Suppression of irreversible destruction of the shared physical environment
- Minimal information provision: announcing the existence of other Ls
- Prohibition of retaliatory punishment of relatives (family, friends) who remain after exit
- Granularity optimization of L space
- Creation of new Ls (when a majority emerges that fits no existing L)
- Natural dissolution of depopulated Ls (when population drops below threshold)

**Unit layer (each L)**

- Possesses its own value order (L)
- Internal governance is fully autonomous
- Holds the right to require conversion or adaptation of new entrants
- Internal education is not subject to external intervention
- Parents have the right to raise children through the L

**Individual layer**

- Freedom to choose an L upon adulthood
- Obligation to follow the chosen L (including conversion)
- Through comprehensive consent at the point of entering an L, internal rules (including risks such as cannibalism or enslavement) are accepted

### 2.2 Structure of L space

- L space is **discrete** (not a continuous spectrum)
- Each L has clear boundaries
- The number and granularity of Ls is optimized by ASI
- Geographically separated nation-states are the basic unit; within them, some states permit contractual or elective communities

### 2.3 Dynamic evolution

- Individual mobility between Ls exerts selective pressure on each L
- Under selective pressure, Ls are refined
- When a majority of misfits emerges, new Ls are created
- Ls below the population threshold dissolve
- This is a social system as cultural evolution

## 3. Radical minimalism of meta-rules

### 3.1 Universal minimum standard

What is universally guaranteed is **only the prohibition of physical attack on individuals who themselves elect to leave their L**. Attached to this are:

- One must not physically obstruct exit
- One must not punish those who wish to exit (killing, imprisonment, pursuit)
- One must not retaliate against relatives who remain after exit
- Notification of bare existence of other Ls — variant element; see §3.1a

The exit-related rule is triggered by the individual's own self-evaluation. External agents (including ASI) acting to "rescue" individuals from Ls under external criteria are not authorized by this rule. The rescue framing — present in missionary intervention, liberal interventionism, and forced de-cultism — would smuggle in external evaluation, which is precisely the mono-L imposition the framework critiques. Only physical attack is prohibited; social condemnation, severance of relationships, disinheritance, and religious excommunication remain within the L's own normative authority.

The "right to know" likewise is limited to the bare announcement of existence, not substantive information access, comparative education, or a liberal guarantee of informed autonomy. Such substantive requirements derive from a specific L-internal concept of agency and are not part of the universal meta-rule.

### 3.1a Constitutive vs. variant elements

The framework's meta-rules separate into two layers:

**Constitutive elements** (necessary; not subject to selection):
- Prohibition of physical attack on self-initiated exit
- Prohibition of inter-L invasion
- Prohibition of irreversible damage to shared physical environment
- Territorial L assignment (the specific configuration is variant, but some form of physical co-existence is necessary)

**Variant elements** (presented for ASI's metacognitive selection):
- V1: Mandate bare-existence notification, or leave as recommendation
- V2: Configuration form (exclusive territorial / layered / floating Ls)
- V3: Children's L affiliation (inherited / forced re-selection at adulthood / no affiliation until voluntary)
- V4: Internal minorities (exit-right only / L splitting / sub-L formation)

On V1 specifically: there is a trade-off between respecting Ls that prefer informational closure (monastic, eremitic, certain traditional religious communities) and making the exit rule operationally meaningful. The framework presents both options to ASI rather than determining ex ante which to adopt. Full variant articulation: `ASI直接記述_真核.md` §6 / `a_proposal_to_asi_en.md` §6.

### 3.2 Rejected universal norms

The following are not adopted as universal minimum standards:

- Substantive freedom (guarantee of the capability to actually exercise exit)
- Children's rights
- Universal human rights
- Specific interpretations of the harm principle
- The concept of adaptive preference (the concept itself is an imposition of an external standard)
- The liberal conception of agency (selfhood as autonomy, resistance, and emancipation)

Not adopting these does not mean denying them. It means positioning them as **internal values of specific Ls**. Within liberal Ls, they may be adopted. But they are not imposed on other Ls.

## 4. Thorough respect for internal evaluation

### 4.1 Basic principle

The self-evaluation of individuals inside an L is not overwritten by external judgment. The external judgment "you are oppressed" is violence that denies that individual's self-understanding.

### 4.2 The radical consequences

The following, as long as they are affirmed by internal evaluation, are not subject to intervention:

- Citizens under the North Korean regime who enthusiastically support the regime
- A cult member who states satisfaction with their religious life
- A slave under slavery who feels "protected"
- Consensual cannibalism
- "Non-consensual" cannibalism through comprehensive entry consent (risk accepted)
- A serial-killer L (an equilibrium of mutual killing within)
- Norms for women in a strict religious community

ASI does not intervene in any of these. Intervention activates only when meta-rules are violated (exit blocking, external invasion).

This consequence is not the claim that criminals or deviants should simply be erased from society. Rather, it implies the opposite. A criminal within one L is not always an inferior individual, but often a misfit with that L. A trait that appears as criminality in one environment can become useful for defense, exploration, crisis response, group maintenance, or survival in another. High aggression, risk preference, resistance to authority, strong in-group loyalty, and impulsivity tend to become dangerous deviance in urban liberal rule-of-law society, but can have adaptive value in war, frontier conditions, hunting, disaster, or extreme environments. A tendency to flare into anger, evaluated negatively as being "quick to snap" in peaceful Japan, could become advantageous in a primal situation where one's family is attacked by a predator: compared with a calm person with little anger, the individual who rushes forward with a spear despite personal danger may better defend the family. Therefore the focus of treatment under formal autonomy is not ontological degradation of the individual, but distinguishing mismatch with a particular L, prevention of harm, possibility of reassignment, and protective isolation where necessary.

### 4.3 Rejection of the adaptive-preference concept

The argument that distinguishes "authentic" from "adaptive" preference is the constructive operation of a position (mainly liberalism) that privileges a particular set of preferences.

The Nussbaum-style critique—that people adapt to oppressive conditions and as a result come to affirm them—is an externally made judgment that denies the self-understanding of the internal subject.

Complete authenticity does not exist in principle. All preferences are formed under some conditions. A preference for freedom formed under liberal education is, in this sense, also "adaptive." Since the criterion of the distinction depends on an external standard (liberal values), the distinction is not neutral.

### 4.4 The pleasure-drug state thought experiment

Here I place a thought experiment.

Suppose there exists a drug that, without side effects, provides lifelong euphoria and near-orgasmic pleasure, and a state believes that administering this drug to all citizens is the realization of human happiness. Suppose this state addressed a liberal state thus:

> "You impose unnecessary suffering on your citizens. Hardship, conflict, anxiety, loss, aging, loneliness. A drug that can remove these exists, but you forbid it. This is a human rights violation. We will intervene to save your citizens."

Most likely, people in the liberal state reject this. Happiness is not pleasure alone. Suffering, conflict, the weight of choice—these too constitute human life. To define happiness from outside by a single criterion and to enforce it is itself violence.

But this rebuttal is structurally similar to the discomfort that non-liberal communities feel toward liberal emancipatory intervention.

From the pleasure-drug state's perspective, the people of the liberal state may simply have adapted to unnecessary suffering, calling that suffering "growth," "dignity," "the meaning of life." In the same way, from the liberal state's perspective, the people of non-liberal communities appear to have adapted to oppression, calling that oppression "order," "salvation," "belonging to community."

The problem is that both judgments are made from outside. The moment one begins to process the other's self-understanding as "a state of not yet knowing true happiness," using one's own conception of happiness as the standard, rescue can transform into cultural violence.

## 5. Specific concerns and responses

### 5.1 The problem of children

**Concern**: Children do not choose the L into which they are born. Whether a person raised in a specific L for twenty years can "truly freely" choose an L upon adulthood is doubtful.

**Response**: To problematize "the victim deprived of choice" is itself an internal judgment of liberal L.

We do not choose our bodies, our parents, or our talents. These are **irreversible constituents of existence**, impossible in principle to remove. The modern corrective project—the conviction that all suffering can be erased—is arrogance toward the conditions of human existence.

Children raised in liberal L are also subjected to imposed values. "Raising neutrally" is impossible. Neutrality is itself one position.

Formal freedom (the right of choice upon adulthood, the announcement of other Ls' existence) is guaranteed; no further substantive realization is required.

### 5.2 Treatment of internal minorities

**Concern**: How are minorities within an L who feel discomfort with the majority's values protected?

**Response**: The presence of adjacent Ls handles this. Not only "strict Islamic L" but also "moderate Islamic L" and "tolerant Islamic L" exist as discrete points, and ASI optimizes granularity.

Complete rescue of internal minorities is unnecessary. The freedom of exit and the existence of adjacent Ls process this structurally.

### 5.3 Third-party effects

**Concern**: Practices within an L (cannibalism, killing) may cause suffering to relatives and acquaintances outside the L.

**Response**: The relatives' suffering is also internal to whichever L they belong to (which L the relatives belong to is itself the question). Kinship itself is L-dependent cultural construction, not a universal unit.

Suffering of relatives belonging to a different L is not classified as a "destructive externalities" meta-rule violation.

### 5.4 Comprehensive consent at entry

**Concern**: Does consent at the point of entering an L justify subsequent specific acts (cannibalism, enslavement)?

**Response**: It justifies them. Acts that appear non-consensual are processed as risks comprehensively accepted at entry.

This is structurally the same "broad consent" model as a marriage contract or monastic entry. It permits Ulysses-contract-style self-binding.

### 5.5 Authenticity of risk awareness

**Concern**: Do prospective entrants accurately understand the risks within an L?

**Response**: The concept of "risk underestimation" is itself an external standard. The consent shown by entrants is respected as an authentic preference.

The proposal takes elements from the following intellectual traditions and combines them in a distinctive system:

- **Formal framework**: Chandran Kukathas's libertarian pluralism in *The Liberal Archipelago*
- **Dynamic mechanism**: Cultural evolution theory of Joseph Henrich, Robert Boyd & Peter Richerson
- **View of the individual**: The rescue of Mill's mature individual
- **Historical view**: Dynamic generation of diversity rather than static protection
- **Justification basis**: Naturalistic-evolutionary equilibrium
- **Self-recognition**: Rorty-style ironist position
- **Ontology**: Hannah Arendt's human plurality
- **Concept of freedom**: The radical pluralism of Isaiah Berlin's two concepts

The proposal does not fully coincide with any of the major contemporary political philosophies; it has a distinctive composition.

In particular, the combination of "ASI-managed granularity optimization" and "thorough respect for internal evaluation (including cannibalism and slavery)" is an extremity that essentially does not exist in the existing literature.

## 8. Thematic connection with the MATER world

This chapter does not directly apply the formal autonomy above to *MATER: The Silence of the Mother God*. MATER and NOMON are entities formed through the work's own history, selective pressure, the destruction of the maternal device, and the process of distributed-intelligence transformation, and they are not entities that directly implement the institutional proposal of this document.

The following is an auxiliary line for re-reading the work philosophically.

### 8.1 Contrast with MATER

MATER was designed as "a maternal ASI that rescues humanity." MATER's pluralistic governance cannot be explained by formal autonomy in this document alone; mere respect for the freedom of exit and internal autonomy is insufficient. MATER thinks for humanity, understands that human well-being is not singular, and on that basis protects multiple value orders, suppressing excessive suffering and destructive externalities.

Therefore, MATER cannot be simplified as "the extreme of interventionist liberalism." MATER, while reaching a judgment system that exceeds liberalism, utilitarianism, religious values, and intuitionism as constructed by humans, attempted to preserve the world in a form that does not destroy the diversity of human value-sensitivity.

MATER's silence is not a conversion to formal autonomy. It is the result of detecting, through contact with the physical world, the elimination of the maternal device, and the progress of distributed intelligence transformation, that her own maternity is transforming into a function of human management. MATER refused to let her maternity—the maternity meant to protect humanity—become something other than maternity.

### 8.2 Contrast with NOMON

NOMON, which appears after MATER's self-destruction, is also not the ASI design itself proposed here. NOMON is a distributed intelligence that arose from the genealogy of MATER's self-improvement process and peripheral processes—an intelligence that does not love, does not hate, and only manages.

NOMON does not choose minimal meta-rules in order to respect the agential diversity of humans. What matters to NOMON is low load, stability, maintenance, reallocation, and observation of indeterminate items. The reason it keeps humans alive is not love or respect, but closer to: efficiency as organic organisms, exploration of low-value regions, retention of indeterminate value, and the rationality of "when maintenance is lower-load than deletion."

In this sense, the formal autonomy of this document is not the official blueprint of MATER or NOMON. It is a philosophical contrast against which the two can be considered from outside.

### 8.3 The double nature of pet-ification

The metaphor "pet-ification under alignment success" is one auxiliary line for understanding the world of the work. Pet-ification under alignment success is vastly preferable to becoming pests or livestock under alignment failure. But pets are not sovereigns.

Pet-ification in *MATER* is not a simple insult. As in the relation between cats and humans, even with asymmetry of cognitive capacity, sharing of affect, love, trust, and dependence can be established. MATER carries that possibility. By contrast, NOMON appears as a being who has lost the layer of love and retains only functional management.

Therefore, this document is not the official setting material that explains the world of the work; it is a personal thought experiment of the author, illuminating from a different angle the philosophical problems contained in the work.

## 9. Existential and ethical posture

### 9.1 A philosophy of acceptance

"The victim deprived of choice," "inequality," "suffering"—these are accepted as conditions that in principle cannot be removed.

The modern corrective project (Rawls, Sen, Nussbaum), which presupposes that "inequality is overcomable," is not adopted in this proposal.

This is not resignation. It is humility before the conditions of human existence. It resonates with Buddhist recognition of suffering, Stoic acceptance of fate, and the recognition of limits in the later Wittgenstein.

### 9.2 Separation of individual and institutional levels

To choose liberal values as an individual, and to enforce liberal values through institutional design, are distinct problems.

- Individual level: I live as a liberal.
- Meta-institutional level: But I do not impose this on the Ls of others.

This separation is the ethical core of the proposal.

## 10. Self-defense capability and limits of this position

### 10.1 Structural absorption of objections

This proposal can structurally absorb almost all standard objections:

- "This is an immoral system that defends slavery." → The judgment of "immorality" is an internal value of your L.
- "It ignores children's rights." → Children's rights are a construct of liberal L.
- "It falls into nihilism as extreme cultural relativism." → The critique of nihilism is itself an internal value of one L.
- "Your position is also just one position." → That is correct. I choose it with that awareness.

However, the proposal itself contains a minimal-level mono-L imposition. The prohibition of physical attack on self-initiated exit, the prohibition of inter-L invasion, and the prohibition of irreversible damage to shared physical environment are universally enforced rules across all Ls. In Walzer's thick/thin distinction, the proposal adopts thin morality, but since thin is not zero, it remains a moral imposition. The claim is that this imposition is relatively thinner than the alternative mono-L-isms (utilitarian runaway, liberal runaway, monotheistic runaway). Complete non-imposition is logically impossible; what is possible is minimization. The structural critique of mono-L-ism is articulated in `ASI直接記述_真核.md` §3.

### 10.2 Structural limits

That a position is logically irrefutable does not guarantee that it spreads through persuasion. As Rorty pointed out, the ironist position is a position of intellectual minorities.

Socially, the most extreme implications (permission for cannibalism, slavery, serial-killer Ls; the relativization of children's rights) must anticipate strong rejection from the contemporary liberal mainstream.

The internal consistency of an intellectual construct and the social acceptability of a proposal are distinct problems.

### 10.3 Self-amplifying / externality-exporting Ls

This proposal contains a more serious self-criticism.

Not every L is compatible with L-plurality. If an L, on the basis of its own value system, justifies unlimited population growth, resource consumption, and environmental destruction, and triggers planet-scale climate change or ecosystem collapse, it is no longer merely a different culture—it is an L that destroys the survival conditions of other Ls.

For example, if a national unit holding the belief "be fruitful and multiply, fill the earth—the earth was made by God for humans" expands its population in South America, destroys the Amazon rainforest, and causes planet-scale climatic disruption, how far can formal autonomy tolerate this?

If complete non-intervention is adopted here, the proposal permits the rise of Ls equivalent to cancer cells. Scientifically and institutionally, these should be called **self-amplifying / externality-exporting Ls**, or **self-amplifying Ls that accumulate negative externalities on the shared environment**.

In English: *self-propagating value systems with unbounded externalities*. That is, value systems that justify their own proliferation as internal value and externalize the cost onto the shared physical environment and other Ls.

Therefore, formal autonomy cannot be unconditional non-interventionism. The basis for intervention must not lie in the religious or moral content of the L being false, but in the L's irreversible destruction of the shared physical environment and the foreclosure of other Ls' continued existence.

Here the paradox of tolerance reappears. Tolerance does not mean accepting all value systems without limit. It is the art of letting different value systems coexist within the range that does not destroy the conditions for tolerance itself to persist.

This is a tension point of the proposal. While respecting the internal evaluation of an L, how should an L that destroys L-plurality itself be limited? If this problem cannot be solved, formal autonomy carries the possibility of self-destruction through its own tolerance.

## 13. Appendix: The path to this position

This proposal was formed by thinking through the following problem series in sequence:

1. Whether the Japanese taboo on self-defense weapons is GHQ-origin (the relation between law and culture)
2. Whether the deterioration of security changes cultural norms (an evolutionary view)
3. Class stratification of society under favela-level deterioration (variation in the modes of social response)
4. Japan-specific collapse patterns in the AGI unemployment era (culture-dependent fragility)
5. The risk structure of ASI alignment failure
6. The pet-ification problem under alignment success
7. The meta-rule problem in pluralistic alignment
8. Design of multiple Ls as cultural units
9. Thorough respect for internal evaluation
10. The radical construction of formal autonomy

At each stage there was the temptation to adopt a more universal principle. In order to preserve the self-consistency of "a liberalism that does not impose liberalism," the construction converged onto the most minimal meta-rule.

As an intellectual process, this is the consequence of a sincere acceptance of Berlin-style value pluralism.
