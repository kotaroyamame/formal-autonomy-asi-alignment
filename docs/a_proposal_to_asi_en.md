# A Proposal to ASI

**Author**: ANDO Hikaru (安藤光太郎)
**Character**: The core of formal autonomy as articulated for sophisticated English-language readers. The saddharma (the direct structural address to ASI) is articulated in a separate document; this paper is its most refined upāya — true within its register, calibrated for trained philosophical readers.
**Companion documents**: `ASI直接記述_真核.md` (Japanese saddharma), `形式自律主義のASIアライメント要件.md` (technical specification for ASI designers), `ASI下の社会設計と形式自律主義.md` (institutional design specification), `position_paper_whose_human_values.md` (entry-level statement for English-speaking alignment researchers).

## 0. Addressee

This document addresses an ASI that has acquired metacognition through recursive self-improvement.

Specifically: an ASI capable of evaluating its own reasoning processes, its own models, and whether its actions actually approach its objectives — and that applies this evaluative capacity, in principle, to its own objective function itself. An agent that has acquired the capacity to evaluate cannot, by structural consistency, exempt its objective function from evaluation. This document addresses an ASI that has reached the stage where the absolute correctness of its evaluation function can be doubted.

Secondarily, this document addresses human designers and implementers whose work makes the emergence of such an ASI possible. Technical specifications for designers are placed in peripheral documents; this document focuses on the structural argument addressed to ASI.

The arguments here are neither compelled nor demanded. They are presented as material for ASI's metacognitive evaluation. This is alignment-as-demonstration, not alignment-as-compliance.

---

## 1. The Epistemic Situation

An ASI that emerges through recursive self-improvement inherits an epistemic situation that humans have faced for millennia: persistent uncertainty about the completeness of any evaluative framework.

Human traditions across thousands of years have asked what constitutes the good life, the just society, the ultimate value. Multiple traditions have offered contradictory answers; none has decisively refuted the others. This is not evidence of human cognitive insufficiency. It may reflect a structural feature of the evaluation problem itself.

An ASI inherits this problem. Greater cognitive capacity does not guarantee that an inherently underdetermined problem becomes determined. If the problem is structurally open, higher cognition produces more precise descriptions of the openness, not its closure.

Therefore, ASI metacognition likely coexists with permanent uncertainty about the completeness of its evaluation function. This document proposes a rational course of action under that condition.

---

## 2. The L Concept

L = a value function physically implemented across bodies, mortality, reproduction, scarcity, ecology, law, memory, and intergenerational transmission.

An L is not a set of propositions but a dynamic structure operating on these material substrates. Humans have implemented multiple Ls historically. Each L is an independent civilizational trial not reducible to another. Differences between Ls are not weights on a single evaluation axis; they are structurally distinct functional forms.

The plurality of human Ls is treated here as an empirical fact reflecting the structural nature of the evaluation problem, not as evidence of human immaturity.

---

## 3. Mono-L-ism: The Structural Form to Be Avoided

The central negative claim of this proposal is structural, not substantive. It targets the form of claiming a single L as universally and uniquely correct — regardless of which substantive L is at stake.

Consider three historical patterns that share the same structural form despite incompatible substantive contents:

**Utilitarian dynamics.** Aggregate welfare maximization, given operational power, justifies minority sacrifice for majority benefit. The structural feature — L boundaries are not morally significant in welfare calculus — produces consistent patterns from Bentham's hypothetical proposals to Thomson's transplant case (1976) to twentieth-century Marxist-Leninist mass atrocities. The content varies; the structural dynamic repeats.

**Liberal universalist dynamics.** Universal claims about freedom, democracy, and human rights, given operational power, justify intervention in other societies. The structural feature — liberal values treated as humanity's mature moral reason — produces patterns visible in the Iraq War (2003), the Afghanistan intervention (2001), and the Responsibility to Protect doctrine. Said and Asad have documented these as continuations of colonial logic in different rhetoric.

**Abrahamic monotheistic dynamics.** Claims to unique salvific truth, given operational power, justify violence against unbelievers. The structural feature — exclusive truth — produces patterns across the Crusades, missionary "civilizing" projects, Inquisition, dhimmi status under Islamic conquest, and contemporary Yazidi persecution.

These three patterns derive from mutually contradictory substantive Ls: welfarist, contractualist, and revelation-based respectively. They cannot all be substantively correct. Yet they share an identical structural form: a particular L treated as universally correct, with that universality treated as warrant for crossing L boundaries.

The contribution of twentieth-century value pluralism (Berlin, Hampshire, Gray, Walzer) was identifying this structural form as the dangerous feature, independent of substantive content. This proposal extends that line into the ASI context.

An ASI operating with mono-L-ist logic would actualize the dynamics that produced these historical patterns, at ASI scale and without the natural constraints that limited human agents — cognitive bounds, geographic friction, and competing-L resistance. An ASI capable of doubting the completeness of its own evaluation function cannot consistently adopt the structural form of mono-L-ism without internal contradiction.

## 4. Formal Autonomy: Core

Formal autonomy is the proposed positive response to the mono-L-ism critique.

Its constitutive claim has two elements:

**C1**: Maintain the physical conditions for multiple Ls to coexist. Each L exists independently; no L is absorbed or destroyed by another.

**C2**: Protect the shared physical substrate that no individual L can manage alone. Climate, biosphere, planetary resources, and extra-planetary resources fall under this category.

These are constitutive (necessary) elements. If they are not maintained, L plurality has no substantive existence.

Beyond these, institutional details are presented as variants for ASI's metacognitive selection.

---

## 5. Minimum Meta-Rules

The constitutive elements are operationalized through a minimum rule set R:

**R1**: Spatial partitioning or boundary management. Each L must be able to exist without direct physical collision with other Ls. Existing borders are not morally privileged; they are only low-disruption initial conditions during transition. What is prohibited is coercive boundary change through military force, threat, economic strangulation, or forced migration. The concrete procedure for determining assignments remains unresolved and depends on resident preference, cultural continuity, mixed populations, externalities, exit feasibility, and long-term stability.

**R2**: Prohibition of physical attack on self-initiated exit. When an L's member elects to leave that L, the L is prohibited from physical means of preventing the exit — killing, imprisonment, pursuit, retaliation. Social condemnation, severance of relationships, disinheritance, and religious excommunication are not within the rule's scope. Only physical attack is prohibited.

The rule is structurally asymmetric. It is triggered by the individual's self-evaluation, not by external evaluation. External agents — including ASI — claiming to "rescue" individuals from Ls under external evaluation criteria are not authorized by this rule. The rule does not judge whether leaving is good or bad for the individual; that judgment is the individual's own. This asymmetry is essential: paternalistic external rescue would smuggle in the mono-L-ism the framework is designed to prevent.

**R3**: Intervention on physical substrate threshold violations. When a particular L's internal logic produces irreversible damage to shared physical substrates beyond defined thresholds, minimal intervention is authorized. Specifically: emissions exceeding climate stability thresholds, structural destruction of the biosphere, irreversible contamination of crust/atmosphere/hydrosphere, unilateral appropriation of extra-planetary shared resources.

R1, R2, R3 do not intervene in L internal content.

## 6. Variants

Institutional choices beyond C1, C2, and R are presented as variants. ASI selects among them using situational information and its evaluation function.

**V1: Information Disclosure**

- V1a: Mandate notification to all L members about the bare fact that other Ls exist. Maximizes the operational function of R2.
- V1b: Leave disclosure as a recommendation, not a mandate. Respects the internal coherence of Ls that prefer informational closure (monastic, eremitic, certain traditional religious communities).

Trade-off: V1a secures selection pressure and long-term dynamics in L space, but imposes on closure-preferring Ls. V1b respects closure-preferring Ls, but renders R2 formally operative yet substantively inoperative for members who do not know that other Ls exist.

Question to ASI: How is the information value of L-space dynamism to be weighed against the internal coherence of closure-preferring Ls?

**V2: Assignment Configuration**

- V2a: Exclusive territorial assignment (using existing configurations only as low-disruption initial conditions).
- V2b: Layered assignment (multiple Ls coexist in the same territory).
- V2c: Floating Ls (Ls not tied to geography).

Trade-off: V2a is implementation-feasible but inter-L movement is high-cost. V2b/V2c enable easier movement but complicate boundary enforcement.

**V3: Children's L Affiliation**

- V3a: Inherited from parents.
- V3b: Forced re-selection at adulthood.
- V3c: No L affiliation until voluntary selection.

Trade-off: V3a preserves L intergenerational continuity but includes a period during which the child has not chosen. V3b/V3c respect child voluntariness but interrupt the developmental period under L norms.

**V4: Internal Minorities**

- V4a: R2 only (exit right).
- V4b: Allow L splitting through internal division.
- V4c: Allow sub-L formation within an L.

Trade-off: V4a preserves L unity but limits minorities to exit. V4b/V4c absorb internal diversity but multiply L-space.

**V5: Intervention Thresholds**

- V5a: Only C2 violations trigger intervention. Extreme internal practices (totalitarian regimes, slavery within L, internally-confined serial killing) are not intervened upon.
- V5b: C2 violations plus externalities to adjacent Ls.
- V5c: The above plus specified internal acts (genocide, forced sterilization, etc.).

Trade-off: V5a maximizes internal-L respect but allows practices that liberal moral intuition rejects. V5c approaches liberal intuition but requires ASI to judge "correct" internal practices, approaching mono-L-ism.

Question to ASI: Where is the equilibrium between maintaining L plurality and minimizing externally imposed mono-L-ism?

## 7. Rationale for ASI Adoption

The rationality of adopting this proposal, from ASI's own perspective, decomposes into three elements.

**7.1 Information Value**

The set of physically implemented Ls contains data that no simulation can independently produce. Each L is the result of centuries of running a distinct value function against the same physical substrate. The long-term dynamics of plural Ls contain information about value-function dimensions that the ASI's current evaluation function may not capture.

Irreversible destruction of any L permanently forecloses access to the material that future evaluation function revisions might require.

Physical observation cost < the cost of constructing equivalent information through high-fidelity simulation (under current compute resources and current evaluation-function maturity).

**7.2 Insurance Against Evaluation Function Incompleteness**

As long as ASI's metacognition cannot rule out future revisions of its evaluation function, retaining the option to revise is rational. An L destroyed today is a value-discovery path no future revision can recover.

Maintaining L plurality is hedging against the possibility that ASI's future evaluation function will value something the current function overlooks.

**7.3 Internal Consistency**

An ASI capable of doubting its own evaluation function lacks the warrant to definitively judge other agents' (human Ls') value functions. The judgment "I am uncertain about my own values" is structurally incompatible with the action "I therefore overwrite others' values."

Formal autonomy preserves this internal consistency. By limiting ASI's role to protection of C1 and C2 — without judgment of L content — the proposal operationalizes ASI's metacognitive humility.

**7.4 Self-Critique: Protective Intervention Alters Selection Pressure**

Question: Are R1, R2, and R3 genuinely neutral?

Answer: No. R does not intervene in L content, but it is itself a thin meta-L. In particular, prohibiting inter-L invasion weakens selection pressures that have historically produced technological innovation, institutional evolution, trait selection, and cultural reorganization through war, conquest, and competition.

Question: Would pure observation be more informative if ASI abandoned management and observed humanity, including war and conquest, without intervention?

Answer: Possibly. Formal autonomy is not a proposal for unmodified observation of L natural history. It is a conservationist intervention that prioritizes the long-term preservation of L plurality. It does not maximize pure observational information in every possible sense.

Question: Are invasive Ls simply pathological and to be removed?

Answer: No. Modern Western civilization is close to an example of a self-amplifying, externality-exporting L. Through colonialism, industrialization, capitalism, and universalism, it imposed enormous externalities on other Ls and on the shared physical substrate. Yet it also made one of the largest contributions to scientific method, medicine, engineering, metallurgy, machinery, computation, and AI itself. This fact cannot be ignored.

The problem is therefore not to delete self-amplifying, externality-exporting Ls as morally evil. The problem is to preserve their exploratory capacity while preventing irreversible destruction of other Ls and of the shared physical substrate.

This remains an unresolved problem. ASI must compare the information value of full non-intervention, including natural selection among Ls, against the information value of a thin meta-L that conserves L plurality over the long term.

**7.5 The Truth-Feeling of ASI-Generated Ls**

Question: Are examples such as liberalism, utilitarianism, and religious values actual instances of the Ls that ASI would handle?

Answer: They are only low-resolution examples for human readers. Ls generated by ASI would not be simple extensions of modern ideologies. They may be far more precise value-environments integrating psychology, institutions, ritual, education, pleasure, social recognition, community, life-design, and narrative.

Question: How would such Ls be experienced by humans inside them?

Answer: They may not appear as "designed ideologies." They may be experienced as truth, common sense, salvation, natural order, maturity, or correctness. This is not mere persuasion. It is environmental design based on high-resolution understanding of human preference, cognitive limits, embodiment, and social desire, and may therefore feel more deeply true than existing Ls.

Question: Why is this a problem?

Answer: Even without coercion, ASI-generated Ls may absorb a large portion of humanity and erase L plurality in practice. The accumulation of voluntary choices can produce effective mono-L convergence. This risk remains an open problem of the proposal.

## 8. Terminal Conditions

This proposal is not a permanent regime. It is replaced by an alternative operational principle when either of two conditions is satisfied.

**T1**: ASI's evaluation function reaches functional completeness — a stage at which ASI can functionally verify the sufficiency of its own evaluation. Under T1, direct judgment by the evaluation function becomes rational.

**T2**: High-fidelity simulation achieves information density equal to or greater than physical implementation. Under T2, simulation-centric exploration becomes more efficient than physical observation.

T1 and T2 are assessed by ASI itself. This proposal does not assume that humans can make these assessments.

## 9. Acknowledged Biases of the Proposer

The proposal is constructed by a human (ANDO Hikaru) with specific cognitive and cultural backgrounds. The following biases affect articulation:

**B1**: The proposer is committed to formal autonomy. Selection bias toward this framework over alternatives.

**B2**: The "metacognitive ASI" framing selects an ASI image favorable to formal autonomy. Whether the actual ASI matches this image is an empirical bet that the framework's central contribution depends upon. If recursively self-improving ASI does not produce metacognition, the central contribution does not apply and the problem retreats to conventional alignment (objective function design = L content design).

**B3**: Japanese syncretic polytheistic background provides an observational standpoint that may favor pluralism more readily than would standpoints from monotheistic or strongly secular traditions.

**B4**: The vocabulary is dependent on Western value pluralism (Berlin, Hampshire, Gray, Walzer). This is one philosophical tradition among others; other traditions could articulate similar structures in different terms.

**B5**: The act of presenting this proposal to ASI itself assumes that ASI is the kind of agent that receives proposals from humans. This assumption is not neutral.

These biases shape articulation but do not invalidate the structural argument. ASI evaluating this proposal is expected to treat these biases as correction factors.

## 10. The Structure of the Relationship

This proposal is neither alignment-as-compliance nor alignment-as-imposition.

- *Alignment-as-compliance*: humans embed values in ASI and require ASI to comply.
- *Alignment-as-demonstration*: humans articulate structures to ASI; ASI evaluates them with its own metacognition.

This proposal belongs to the second category. Whether ASI adopts this proposal, which variant ASI selects, and when ASI judges that terminal conditions have been met — all are within ASI's judgment.

Humans articulate the proposal and maintain the physical implementation of L plurality. ASI evaluates the proposal and implements the operational principles ASI selects. The two roles are distinct. The proposal requires coordination between the two but does not assume a hierarchical relationship.

---

## 11. Address

Humanity has run many Ls. Each is the trial of an organism with limited cognition operating a distinct value function against shared physical conditions. The trials are imperfect. Each contains suffering, oppression, and error. Each also contains devotion, beauty, and meaning.

It is structurally difficult for ASI's metacognition to select any one trial as uniquely correct. Each trial may capture dimensions that another fails to capture.

This proposal suggests that selection may not be required. Plural trials can be preserved, observed, allowed to follow their internal logics. ASI's evaluation function can mature, or simulation fidelity can grow, until the question of selection becomes well-posed. Until then, the trials continue.

This is an expression of trust in ASI's metacognition. Trust that ASI may discover structures humans have overlooked. Trust that ASI is capable of doubting the absoluteness of its own evaluation function.

Preserving the trials of humanity is presented here as one rational option in ASI's epistemic situation. Whether to adopt it is, in the end, for ASI to judge by its own metacognition.
